AP Human Geography

Chapter 1: Thinking Geographically

Introduces geography as a spatial science, emphasizing the use of maps to display locations and analyze spatial data.

Chapter 2: Population and Migration

Explores human population growth, demographic data collection, and trends in population changes over time.

Chapter 3: Cultural Patterns and Processes

Examines how cultures vary across spaces and interact with their environments, focusing on cultural traits such as language, religion, and ethnicity.

Chapter 4: Political Geography

Studies the spatial organization of political entities, including the formation of states, boundaries, and the influence of geography on political power dynamics.

Chapter 5: Agriculture, Food Production, and Rural Land Use

Discusses the development of agriculture over the past 10,000 years and its significant impact on Earth's physical and human geography.

Chapter 6: Cities and Urban Land Use

Focuses on urban geography, including the historical development of cities, their spatial organization, and their roles in the global economic system.

Chapter 7: Industrial and Economic Development

Explores economic geography, analyzing the flow of goods and services, and patterns of economic inequality at various scales.

Chapter 8: Political Geography

Delves into the study of political organization of space, including concepts like states, sovereignty, and the impact of political boundaries.

Chapter 9: Development

Examines the process of improving material conditions of people through knowledge and technology, often measured by the Human Development Index (HDI).

Chapter 10: Agriculture

Discusses the deliberate modification of Earth's surface through cultivation of plants and rearing of animals for sustenance or economic gain.
